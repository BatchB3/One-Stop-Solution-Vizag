The temple is a perfect blend of traditional Vedic and non-Vedic cultures. However, one can safely say that the idol of the Mother Goddess dates back to the Chalukya-Chola period, with the sculptural features clearly indicating that the idol could have belonged to a temple dating back to the 12th century AD.

During the annual jathara, which is conducted immediately after Sankranthi in January, the Goddess can be seen at her decorated best.

The temple has a 600-year old history when it was discovered by fishermen. The goddess appeared in a dream and instructed that the idol is handed over to the Maddi family. Since then, the temple has been under the care of the Maddi family.
