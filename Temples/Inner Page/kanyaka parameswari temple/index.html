Vasavi Maatha is hailed with veneration for promoting peace and Ahimsa during the 4000 BC . She is credited with averting war and thereby saving many lives through logic and reason and overcoming brute force.

This version has further evolved in recent years where the orthodox sections have dropped even the name Vasavi and only call her Kanyaka Parameshwari which is a very violent form of Parvati with 10 hands armed with multiple weapons like swords, spears, tridents, chakras who is blood thirsty and one of her many hands hold the severed head of the king, She is seated on a ferocious lion whose mouth is dripping with blood and human flesh pouncing on the decapitated body of King Vishnu Vardhana. Orthodox Arya Vysyas offer many kinds of Vedic sacrifices which includes killing of animals to appease the violent form of the goddess.
